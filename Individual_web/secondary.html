MY SECONDARY SCHOOL

SEKOLAH MENENGAH KEBANGSAAN SULTAN ABDUL AZIZ SHAH, KAJANG

Sekolah Menengah Sultan Abdul Aziz Shah, Kajang began in 1959 with the establishment of the Malay secondary degree. As the Malay middle school in the district first, the establishment of Malay secondary degree is closely related to the development of Malay language medium education in the country in line with the development of national education, namely education for all people to use the Malay language as the main medium.

At first, the Malay secondary degree has no established his own school building. Until 1960, the school occupied the building of the Kajang Women's National Primary School.

In the early 1960s, Standard Malay secondary growth with an increase in the number of classes and students. The response of the people in this area Malay secondary degree so good. The situation prompted the government to build a secondary school at Batu 14, Jalan Cheras, Kajang on a 10.23-acre site. At the end of 1963, the school was completed and in January 1964, the school was opened in the name of Malay secondary school to live with a guy Heights Primary School moved to this school.

At that time, SMKSAAS was the first secondary school in Kajang. SMKSAAS started with five classes consisting of 326 students and eight teachers. These five classes are in grade four. This means that students who have graduated from LCE will no longer need to continue their studies in Kuala Lumpur.
